About

I am an IT Service Management leader with 25+ years in enterprise IT — the last twelve as a Vice President at a Fortune 500 global investment bank, leading global teams across the US, EMEA, and Asia. I work hands-on across the full ITIL lifecycle: governance, Incident, Problem, Change, Release, Configuration, Asset, and Major Incident Management. My focus is building governance controls and observability directly into service workflows, so that reliability and compliance are designed in rather than inspected in — and service performance becomes measurable in terms that matter to the business.

How I Think

I experienced 9/11 while working at the World Bank. Since then, resiliency has been part of my design — I design with failure in mind, because that is what makes systems stable. My stance is a balance between reliability and validity: proven practice where it protects the business, exploration where it creates value.

In a regulated industry, exploration doesn't mean breaking rules — it means recombining them to create unique solutions. And the best solutions start with empathy: service blueprints, listening, and human-centered design thinking, so we build what users actually need.
